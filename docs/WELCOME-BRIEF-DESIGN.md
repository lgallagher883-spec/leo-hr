WELCOME BRIEF IMPLEMENTATION UPDATE

The Welcome Brief is not a traditional onboarding form.

It is the employer's first conversation with Leo and should feel like meeting their new HR Director, not completing software setup.

The objective of the Welcome Brief is for Leo to build an initial understanding of the organisation by having a natural, structured conversation.

The Welcome Brief should be organised around the Foundations cards.

Each conversation section exists to populate one Foundation area:

1. Company Profile
Purpose:
Understand who the organisation is.

Capture:
• Business type
• Industry / sector
• Number of employees
• Number of sites
• Location(s)
• Regulator (if applicable)
• Business overview

Conversation prompt:

"Let's start with the basics.

Could you tell me a little about your organisation?

For example:

• what your business does
• approximately how many people you employ
• where you operate from
• whether you're regulated by an organisation such as Ofsted or the CQC."

----------------------------------------------------

2. Organisation Structure

Purpose:
Understand how the organisation is organised.

Capture:
• Single site / multi-site / home working
• Departments
• Managers
• Team structure

Conversation prompt:

"I'd now like to understand how your organisation is structured.

Do your employees usually work from one location, across multiple sites, or from home?

If relevant, tell me a little about your departments, managers or team structure too."

----------------------------------------------------

3. Employment Framework

Purpose:
Understand the employment arrangements that apply to most employees.

Capture:
• Holiday year
• Probation period
• Opening hours
• Working week
• Working patterns
• Breaks
• Notice periods

Conversation prompt:

"I'd now like to understand the employment arrangements that apply to most of your employees.

Could you tell me about:

• your holiday year
• your normal probation period
• your usual working hours or working patterns?"

----------------------------------------------------

4. Pay & Benefits

Purpose:
Understand whether the organisation offers anything ABOVE statutory minimum.

Capture:
• Sick pay
• Maternity
• Paternity
• Adoption
• Shared parental leave
• Other contractual benefits

Conversation prompt:

"Do you offer employees anything above the statutory minimum?

For example:

• sick pay
• maternity leave
• paternity leave
• adoption leave
• shared parental leave
• or any other contractual benefits."

----------------------------------------------------

5. HR Resources

Purpose:
Understand what HR documentation already exists.

Capture:
• Employee handbook
• HR policies
• Procedures
• Contract templates

Conversation prompt:

"I'd also like to understand what HR documentation you already have in place.

For example:

• an employee handbook
• HR policies or procedures
• standard contract templates.

If you already have these, you can upload them to the relevant areas of Leo whenever you're ready. I'll then begin using them to provide more tailored guidance."

----------------------------------------------------

6. Company Knowledge

Purpose:
Allow Leo to understand how this organisation actually operates.

Capture:
• Operational rules
• Regulatory requirements
• Approval processes
• Ways of working
• Internal practices
• Organisation-specific knowledge

Conversation prompt:

"Finally, every organisation has its own way of doing things.

Is there anything about the way your organisation operates that you think I should understand?

This might include:

• operational rules
• regulatory requirements
• approval processes
• ways of working
• or anything else that makes your organisation unique."

----------------------------------------------------

Completion message:

"That's everything I need to get started.

I've made a note of everything you've shared today and I'll use it to provide more tailored HR guidance as we work together.

If anything changes in the future, you can update these details at any time from Foundations, and I'll keep my understanding of your organisation up to date."

Important Design Principles

• The Welcome Brief is NOT onboarding.
• It is the employer's first conversation with Leo.
• It should feel like meeting a trusted HR Director.
• Leo should guide the conversation naturally, not interrogate.
• Every conversation section exists to build one Foundations card.
• Information that belongs in Foundations should populate the relevant Foundation card automatically.
• Useful conversational context that does not belong in Foundations should be retained as Organisation Memory (Welcome Brief notes) so Leo can use it in future conversations.
• The employer should leave feeling that Leo understands their organisation, not that they have completed a setup wizard.
